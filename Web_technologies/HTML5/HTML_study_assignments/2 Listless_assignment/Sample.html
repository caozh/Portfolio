Famous Quote

Everyone has a set of famous quotes that inspire, motivate, or just amuse them. Keeping in mind that there is no explaining humor, here is one of my all-time favorite quotes. ( I apologize in advance should you find this offensive in any way!)

This is a famous, albeit perfectly bizarre quote from American History. It is attributed to the judge who sentenced Alfred Packer after Packer's conviction of cannibalism. Packer led a group of settlers through the Donner Pass and was trapped in a snowstorm. Allegedly, Packer emerged none the worse from wear, and his deceased travel companions were presumed to have unwillingly contributed to his survival.  (If you want to know about the story before you read the quote, click here to see a copy of the Wikipedia entry for Packer.) The story, if true, is of course morally repugnant. What makes the story even more bizarre is that Packer's sentencing judge apparently interpreted these foul events not as a repulsed human being, but as an outraged Democrat.

The quote is replete with cursing and vulgar language, which I abhor. However, it is included in unedited form below:

Stand up yah voracious man-eatin' sonofabitch and receive yir sitince. When yah came to Hinsdale County, there was siven dimmycrats. But you, yah et five of 'em, goddam yah. I sintince yah t' be hanged by th' neck ontil yer dead, dead, dead, as a warnin' ag'in reducin' th' Dimmycratic populayshun of this county. Packer, you Republican cannibal, I would sintince ya ta hell but the statutes forbid it.

Packer Biography

Packer was born (as Alfred G. Packer) in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania. He served in the American Civil War, on the Union side presumably in an Iowa regiment, but was mustered out due to epilepsy. In November, 1873, Packer was with a party of 21 who left Provo, Utah, bound for the Colorado gold country in Breckenridge. On January 21, 1874 he met with Chief Ouray (known as the White Man's Friend) near Montrose, Colorado. Chief Ouray recommended they postpone their expedition until spring, as they were likely to encounter dangerous winter weather in the mountains. In spite of Ouray's advice, a party of six that included Packer left for Gunnison, Colorado on February 9. The other five men were Shannon Wilson Bell, James Humphrey, Frank "Reddy" Miller, George "California" Noon and Israel Swan. At an unknown date, the party got hopelessly lost, ran out of provisions, and became snowbound in the Rocky Mountains. Packer allegedly went scouting and came back to discover Bell roasting human meat. According to Packer, Bell rushed him with a hatchet; Packer shot and killed him.
